Details

What?

hackXchange is a hackathon for high schoolers in Singapore. A hackathon is a competition during which students who are interested in exploring computer science or engineering team up and build cool projects (apps, games, robots, or whatever they're excited about). hackXchange is open to students with a variety of skill levels -- no prior knowledge or experience needed. All participants will have a chance to pitch to sponsors and judges to win prizes; beginners will also be provided with workshops and special prizes.

When?

hackXchange is a 12-hour hackathon, from 10 AM to 10 PM, on June 8th. We'll be sending out a more detailed schedule closer to the event, but we're planning to have a few opening remarks, at least 10 hours of dedicated time for students to work on their projects (and eat -- lunch and snacks are provided!), and a science-fair style expo presentation and award ceremony at the end of the event.

Frequently asked questions

What happens at a hackathon?

During a hackathon, participants ("hackers") work in teams of up to 4 to build projects ("hacks") from scratch. These projects can be pretty much anything: apps, games, robots, websites, or anything else you can dream up!

Do I need a team or an idea?

Not at all! You're welcome to start brainstorming or forming a team ahead of time, but this is certainly not required. You will be able to find a team and come up with an idea at the event.

Is there a fee to attend?

Absolutely not! hackXchange is completely free to attend, and thanks to the generous support of our sponsors, we'll also have lunch and snacks provided throughout the day.

What should I bring?

All you need to bring is a student ID, a laptop, and an open mind! If you are planning on creating a hardware hack, you are responsible for providing your own materials.

What do people usually make?

There are very few limits on what you can build, but here are some examples of what people have made at other hackathons. Keep in mind that almost all of these projects were created by college students during longer events (typically 36 hours) -- your projects at hackXchange do not have to be this elaborate!

What can I win?

We'll have several prizes at the event, including overall prizes (1st, 2nd, 3rd) and prizes for hacks in specific areas. We'll also have a special prize specifically for beginners. Specifics about prizes will be released closer to the event.

What resources will be available?

We'll have mentors available to help you with various aspects of your project, like iPhone app development or circuit design, as well as optional workshops centered around specific topics, such as web design or backend development.

How do I sign up?

You can register for hackXchange as a participant, mentor, or volunteer by filling out this form.

Isn't hacking illegal?

In this context, "hacking" means quickly and cleverly building a piece of hardware or software from scratch. This is completely different from illegal hacking, which involves gaining unauthorized access to a computer system.

Who can attend?

hackXchange is open to all high school students. If you're younger, email us and we'll see what we can do. If you're older, please consider registering as a mentor or volunteer instead.

What if I've never coded before?

No problem! One of our main goals is to provide an opportunity for students with little exposure to computer science to see how fun and powerful it can be. If you're a beginner, we'll have several introductory workshops teaching you how to code something from scratch. We'll also award a special "Best Beginner Project" prize at the end of the event.
